Potato soup

I only want soup when it's cold. Why? I don't know. Because it's warm and makes my body warm, and when it's hot in Georgia I don't want to be more hot? You figure it out. It's not important.

What is important is to know that this soup is good and hearty and great. It's cooked in bacon fat, so that's always a plus. I got the recipe from Delish.

Ingredients

  • 6 slices bacon, cut into 1/2" pieces
  • 1 large yellow onion, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 cloves garlic, finely chopped
  • 1 tsp fresh thyme leaves
  • 4 cups low-sodium chicken broth
  • 2 1/2 lb russet potatoes peeled & cut into 1/2" cubes, about 5 medium
  • 1 1/2 cups half-and-half
  • Kosher salt
  • freshly ground black pepper
  • 1/2 cup sour cream, plus more for serving
  • Shredded cheddar cheese, for serving

Instructions

  1. Set Instant Pot to "Saut√©" setting. Cook bacon, stirring occasionally, until crisp, 10 to 12 minutes. Using a slotted spoon, transfer bacon to a plate, leaving fat in pot.
  2. Add onion and cook, stirring occasionally, until softened, 5 to 7 minutes. Stir in flour, garlic, and thyme and cook, stirring occasionally, until fragrant, 1 minute more. Pour in broth and stir until flour is dissolved, about 30 seconds.
  3. Add potatoes, half-and-half, 1 1/2 teaspoons salt, and 1/4 teaspoon pepper and stir to combine. Cover pot, then set to Pressure Cook on High and set timer for 8 minutes. Slow release pressure.
  4. Uncover pot and stir in sour cream. Using an immersion blender, blend until some chunky bits of potato remain. Alternatively, you can use a standard blender to blend about half of soup and return to pot.
  5. Divide soup among bowls. Top with more sour cream, bacon, cheddar, and chives.
